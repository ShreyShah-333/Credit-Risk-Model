1. Default Rate by Grade

Grade is the master variable. Grade A=6.1% vs Grade G=50.1% — 8x difference. Interest rate directly prices the default risk.

2. Default Rate by Loan Purpose

Small business (29.7%) is riskiest. Wedding (12.2%) and car (14.6%) are safest. Debt consolidation (21.3%) is deceptive — largest category but above-average risk.

3. Loan Term & DTI Analysis

60-month loans default TWICE as often as 36-month (32.6% vs 16.1%). DTI is almost perfectly linear — every 5% increase adds 2-3% default risk.

4. Vintage Analysis

2016 was the worst vintage (24.3%) — lending standards loosened at peak growth. DTI crept from 10.3% (2007) to 18.8% (2016) — directly causing more defaults.

5. Recovery Analysis

Overall recovery rate is only 7.5% — net loss is 92.5% of defaulted amount. Grade A has LOWEST recovery (5.8%) — sophisticated borrowers use bankruptcy. Total net losses: $3.76B across 261,655 defaulted loans.

6. Early Warning Signals

Hard inquiries: 0 inquiries=18% default, 5 inquiries=28.2% — strong behavioral signal. Home ownership: MORTGAGE=17.3% vs RENT=23.3% — 6% spread. Utilisation above 80% signals financial stress.
